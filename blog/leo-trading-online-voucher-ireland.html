How to Get the LEO Trading Online Voucher for Your Irish Business

Avenor Team
Published • July 2026
TL;DR The Local Enterprise Office (LEO) in Ireland offers a €2,500 grant (the Trading Online Voucher) to help small businesses build or upgrade their e-commerce capabilities. You must have fewer than 10 employees, be trading for 6 months, and attend a mandatory seminar. A high-converting e-commerce site pays for itself, and Avenor can provide the necessary LEO-compliant quotes and build your compliant e-commerce site.

If you own a small business in Ireland and want to start selling your products or services online, the financial cost of a high-quality e-commerce website can seem daunting. Between design, development, payment gateway integration, and SEO, building a site that actually converts visitors into paying customers requires a significant investment.

Fortunately, the Irish government provides incredible support through the Local Enterprise Office (LEO). The Trading Online Voucher Scheme (TOVS) is one of the most accessible and valuable grants available to Irish SMEs. It offers financial assistance of up to €2,500 (with co-funding of 50% from the business) to help you develop or upgrade your e-commerce website.

In this comprehensive 2026 guide, we will walk you through exactly what the voucher is, who is eligible, what it covers, and the exact step-by-step process you need to follow to secure your funding and launch your digital storefront.

1. Why the Trading Online Voucher Exists

The Irish government recognizes that the future of retail and B2B sales is digital. However, many small, traditional businesses lack the upfront capital required to digitize their operations. The Trading Online Voucher was created to bridge this gap, encouraging small businesses to reach new markets—both domestically and internationally—by selling online.

Businesses that have utilized the voucher report average sales increases of 20%, with an 80% jump in business inquiries. More importantly, 3 in 5 businesses begin exporting for the first time after launching their e-commerce platform.

2. Are You Eligible?

Before you begin the application process, you must ensure your business meets the strict eligibility criteria set by LEO. The scheme is aimed at micro-enterprises. Your business must:

  • Have no more than 10 employees: This grant is specifically for small businesses.
  • Have less than €2 million in turnover: Larger enterprises are not eligible.
  • Be trading for at least 6 months: Startups in the ideation phase cannot apply; you must have an established trading history.
  • Be located in the area covered by the specific LEO: You must apply to the LEO office in your specific county or city jurisdiction.

It is also worth noting that businesses can now apply for a second Trading Online Voucher if they have already successfully utilized their first one and need to further upgrade their e-commerce capabilities.

3. What Can the Voucher Be Used For?

The grant is designed specifically to help you trade online. It cannot be used simply to build an "online brochure" (a website that only displays information without a transaction capability). Permitted expenses include:

  • IT Consultation and Web Development: Paying an agency like Avenor to design and code your website.
  • E-commerce Upgrades: Adding a shopping cart, payment gateways (like Stripe or PayPal), or booking systems to an existing website.
  • App Development: If it directly facilitates online trading.
  • Digital Marketing Strategy: Up to 30% of the approved voucher costs can be used for digital marketing strategy and execution (e.g., SEO, Google Ads setup).
  • Training: Training your staff on how to manage the new e-commerce CMS or digital marketing tools.

What is NOT Covered?

You cannot use the voucher for the development of brochure websites, purchase of non-internet related software, hardware purchases (like laptops or servers), or paying for existing ongoing subscriptions (like monthly hosting fees).

4. The Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Attend the Mandatory Information Seminar

You cannot simply fill out a form online and receive the funds. You are absolutely required to attend a Trading Online Voucher information seminar hosted by your local LEO. These are usually half-day seminars (currently often held online via Zoom) and provide excellent insights into what is required to trade online successfully. You will receive your application form at or after this seminar.

Step 2: Get Three Detailed Quotes

To ensure fair market value and prevent fraud, LEO requires you to submit three distinct quotes from web development agencies. The quotes must be highly detailed. A single line item saying "Website - €5,000" will be rejected.

The quotes must break down the costs into specific categories, such as UX/UI Design, Front-End Development, Back-End E-commerce Integration, Payment Gateway Setup, and SEO. Note: At Avenor, we frequently provide detailed, LEO-compliant quotes for Irish businesses seeking this voucher. We know exactly how to format these to pass the LEO review process.

Step 3: Write Your Business Proposal

Along with the quotes, you must submit a detailed proposal explaining how an e-commerce website will help your business grow. You need to outline:

  • Your Target Market: Who are you selling to? Are you expanding from local to national, or national to international?
  • Expected ROI: How much do you expect your sales to increase?
  • Your Digital Marketing Strategy: How will you drive traffic to the new website once it is built?

Step 4: Approval and Drawdown

Once your application is approved by the LEO evaluation committee, you will receive a Letter of Offer. Do not start work or pay your web developer before receiving this letter. If you pay for the work before approval, those costs are ineligible.

After the work is completed and you have paid your developer in full, you submit the invoices and proof of payment to LEO, and they will reimburse you for 50% of the approved costs, up to €2,500.

5. Why Your E-commerce Site Needs to be Custom

When applying for the voucher, you might be tempted to hire the cheapest developer who will just throw your products onto a basic Shopify or WooCommerce template. However, you are investing your own money alongside the government's. You need a return on this investment.

A custom-built e-commerce site ensures lightning-fast load times (crucial for mobile shoppers), a frictionless checkout process that reduces cart abandonment, and a unique brand experience that builds trust with Irish consumers.

Conclusion: Take the Leap

The Trading Online Voucher is a fantastic initiative to bring your Irish business into the modern digital economy. A high-converting, fast, and SEO-optimized e-commerce site will generate returns far beyond the initial co-funded investment.
